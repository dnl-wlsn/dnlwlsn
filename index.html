My career (so far)

SEGA

My first testing role was at SEGA, straight after graduating. I studied Digital Music & Computer Games Design at University and, like most teenagers, loved playing games... which definitely helped in getting a job here.

SQS

After meeting a real(!) software tester, I applied for a role at Software Quality Systems and was sent to South Africa for three weeks, had a crash course in testing, and passed my ISTQB Foundation.

HRG, GC, TF

Whilst at the consultancy, I worked for the Home Retail Group (retail), Gala Coral (gaming / gambling), and Think Finance (...finance, obviously). I was then poached by Think Finance and started work there permanently.

Think Finance

Absolutely loved it here. We were a small team doing some big work, was learning loads, and it felt like family. I was here for two years before venturing in to contracting, thinking the grass could be greener

Oakam

Oakam was my first contracting role and a real lesson in life. There's no safety net in contracting, no real perks... just lots of sleepless nights and paperwork. I was here for six months before going heading to back to Think Finance.

Elevate

Think Finance had a name change whilst I was gone, but the startup feel and family vibe was still there. I was contracting here for a further 1.5 years until heading off to my final contracting role.

Cabot

I didn't really know it at the time, but Cabot would be my final contract role. I found that contractors were treated differently and I was more interested in permanency.

Connells

It was harder going back to permanent work because companies assumed you'd dump them as soon as another contract came along. Unfortunately, Connells was just too slow-paced and I didn't really enjoy it.

NewDay

I was referred to NewDay and took a step in to credit risk testing. It was fun here - the offices were awesome and the people were great. However, credit risk testing wasn't really for me...

Simply Business

I was referred to Simply Business and found a new family. I really love it here, but there's no room for progression... which is why I'm open to new roles and probably why you're reading this!
